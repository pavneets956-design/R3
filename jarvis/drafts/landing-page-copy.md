### FAQ Section (/)

**Q: What does R3 do?**
A: R3 is a browser extension that displays subreddit rules and posting requirements directly on Reddit. You see the rules without leaving the page or the composer.

**Q: Is it free?**
A: The core extension is free. A Pro tier with risk scoring and removal detection is coming in a future update.

**Q: What data does R3 collect?**
A: Nothing leaves your device. R3 stores your subreddit notes and cached rules locally in your browser. It does not track you, transmit your data, or use analytics. See the privacy policy for full details.

**Q: Does it work on mobile?**
A: Not yet. R3 is a browser extension — it works on Chrome and Edge on desktop. Mobile support is not in the current roadmap.

**Q: Does it work on old Reddit?**
A: Not in Phase 1. R3 targets New Reddit (reddit.com). Old Reddit support may come in a later version.

**Q: How do I delete my data?**
A: Go to Chrome Settings > Extensions > R3 > Clear storage. Or uninstall the extension — all locally stored data is deleted.

## Feature Page: /reddit-post-removed-without-notice

**Title tag:** Why Reddit Removes Posts Without Telling You — And How to Know | R3

**Meta description:** Reddit silently removes 69% of posts without notification. R3's removal detection (coming in Pro) shows whether your post is actually visible to other users.

**H1:** Reddit removed your post. You just don't know it yet.

When Reddit removes a post — by moderator action, automod, or spam filter — it usually does not notify you. Your post looks live to you. To everyone else, it's gone.

This affects new accounts disproportionately. Karma thresholds, account age filters, and automod rules are enforced silently and frequently.

**R3 addresses this two ways:**

First, the free rules display tells you about known restrictions before you post. If a subreddit requires 100 karma and you have 45, you see that before hitting submit.

Second, R3 Pro (coming in Phase 2) adds real-time removal detection. Once you've posted, R3 checks whether your post is visible to logged-out users — the standard indicator of whether it's been removed.

## Chrome Web Store Description (adapted)

**Short description (60 chars):**
See subreddit rules and post intelligence before you submit.

**Full description:**

R3 surfaces subreddit rules and posting requirements directly inside Reddit — while you browse and while you compose.

WHAT R3 DOES IN PHASE 1:

- Displays subreddit rules from Reddit's live API in a floating panel
- Updates automatically when you navigate between subreddits
- Saves per-subreddit notes in your browser (locally — nothing transmitted)
- Shows your detected Reddit username in the panel header

COMING IN R3 PRO (PHASE 2):

- Risk scoring: check your account stats against the subreddit's karma and age requirements before you post
- Removal detection: find out if your post is visible to other users or was silently removed

PRIVACY:

R3 stores data locally only. No server. No analytics. No account required. Subreddit rules are fetched directly from Reddit's public API by your browser. Your notes stay on your device.

PERMISSIONS:

- storage: to save your notes and cache rules locally
- reddit.com access: to inject the panel and fetch rules

R3 is honest about what is live vs. what is coming. The risk and removal features are clearly labeled "Pro — coming soon" inside the extension.
